The Pain Point

From your integration map and our kickoff call, one gap stands above the rest: the VA/Admin Audit Layer. The systems record what happens — but nobody verifies daily that what should happen, did. This engagement fills that role, and in doing so absorbs three related gaps from your map.

Absorbed 01

Daily verification

Lead follow-ups, booking confirmations, onboarding deadlines, attendance, and check-ins — verified every day per trainer and per client, not discovered weeks later.

Absorbed 02

Session-balance + renewal tracking

Clients running low on sessions are flagged before they hit zero, and every renewal outcome — renewed, lapsed, win-back — gets recorded and reported.

Absorbed 03

Red-flag escalation

Missed check-ins, no-shows, and unhappy-client signals get logged in Monday the same day with a named owner — nothing depends on someone happening to notice.

Explicitly out of scope: form builds, funnel builds, and platform reconfiguration. Your systems stay exactly as they are — this layer audits and reports on top of them.

GoHighLevel

GymMaster

Trainerize
I audit

Monday

The one rule: I audit and report — I don't edit data outside its home system. Attendance stays GymMaster's record, check-ins stay Trainerize's. What I add is the daily verification that those records are complete, accurate, and acted on.

Lifecycle Checkpoints

Your Team Wireframe already defines a VA/Admin checkpoint at every stage of the client lifecycle. Here's what gets verified at each one, and what escalates if it slips. Click a stage to explore.

Acquire

Audited in: GoHighLevel

Every inbound lead gets a response-speed audit: did the follow-up fire, how fast, and is the pipeline stage accurate? Leads that sit unanswered are the most expensive silence in the business — this checkpoint makes sure it never lasts past a day.

I verifyLead response speed, follow-up sequences firing, source attribution and pipeline stage matching reality.
Escalates whenA lead sits past the agreed response window with no follow-up → logged in Monday with an owner, same day.

Book Intro

Audited in: GoHighLevel + GymMaster

Every intro-week booking gets confirmed: reminder sent, appointment on the trainer's schedule, no double-bookings or orphaned slots.

I verifyBooking confirmations and reminders sent; GHL bookings matching GymMaster's schedule.
Escalates whenA booked lead has no confirmed appointment → flagged before the slot is lost.

Convert

Audited in: GymMaster + Stripe

Every sale gets a three-way match: the package the client chose, the payment that actually processed, and the client record they're attached to. Billing errors caught on day one instead of at month-end.

I verifyPackage, payment, and client ID all match; recurring billing set correctly; conversion marked in GHL.
Escalates whenA sale is recorded without verified payment or package → flagged to management same day.

Onboard

Audited in: Trainerize + Monday

Your 24-hour onboarding standard, enforced. Every new client's app setup, intake form, and plan delivery are checked against the clock — nothing depends on a coach remembering.

I verifyApp invite accepted, intake form completed, nutrition + mobility plan delivered within 24 hrs.
Escalates whenOnboarding incomplete past 24 hrs → escalated to management with what's missing and who owns it.

Deliver + Monitor

Audited in: Trainerize + GymMaster

The heart of the daily cadence. Per trainer and per client: attendance logged, check-ins completed, sessions counted, red flags spotted. This is where "what's done vs. what's in progress" lives — reported daily, rolled up weekly.

I verifyAttendance and no-shows, weekly check-in completion, session balances, survey responses, and red-flag signals.
Escalates whenMissed check-in, no-show, or unhappy-client signal → logged in Monday same day with a named owner.

Renew + Grow

Audited in: GymMaster + Monday

Session balances are watched continuously so the renewal conversation happens while there's momentum — never after a lapse. Every renewal outcome gets recorded: renewed, lapsed, or win-back, with offboard notes when a client leaves.

I verifyClients approaching zero sessions flagged early; renewal outcomes and win-back / offboard notes recorded for every expiring package.
Escalates whenA renewal window passes with no recorded outcome → flagged in the weekly report and Monday.

Audit & Report Cadence

The core of this engagement: a running rhythm of audits and reports per trainer and per client — what's done, what's in progress, who's running low on sessions, and how clients are feeling.

Daily coverage commitment: at least 6 hours per day across three blocks — start of day, mid-day, and end of day — extended whenever the day demands it.
Start of day
2 hrs

Open the day clean

Review overnight leads and client check-ins, confirm today's sessions and bookings per trainer, flag onboarding deadlines due today, and surface anything that would block a trainer's first session.

Mid-day
2 hrs

Done vs. in progress

Per-trainer status pass: what's completed, what's in progress, what's slipping. Log morning attendance and no-shows, run the session-balance check to flag clients running low, and chase pending follow-ups.

End of day
2 hrs

Close and prep

Close out the day per trainer and per client, log every exception into Monday with a named owner, send the end-of-day status summary, and prep tomorrow's priority list.

Weekly

Trainer + client reports

Per trainer: sessions delivered, check-ins completed, red flags raised. Per client: attendance, check-in completion, sessions remaining, and renewals coming up in the next 30 days.

Monthly

Feedback + business review

Client feedback check-ins and surveys, retention / churn / capacity review, and the owner summary report — what the numbers say and what to do about them.

Always on

Session-balance alerts

Clients approaching zero sessions are flagged before they run out — triggering the renewal conversation while there's still momentum, never after a lapse.

Rollout Plan

Four steps to a fully running audit layer. No platform changes at any step — just access, verification, and reporting.

Step 1
Week 1

Access + baseline audit

Access granted across GHL, GymMaster, Trainerize, and Monday. First pass documents the current state per trainer and per client — session balances, check-in completion, pending onboarding — and surfaces any backlog issues already sitting in the systems.

Gate: baseline report delivered — where everything stands today.
Step 2

Daily cadence live

The three-block daily routine begins: start-of-day, mid-day, and end-of-day checks, with every exception logged in Monday and an end-of-day summary sent.

Gate: one full week of daily audits with EOD summaries delivered.
Step 3

Weekly + monthly reports live

Report formats agreed with you, then delivered on schedule: weekly per-trainer and per-client reports, monthly feedback check-ins, and the retention/churn review.

Gate: first weekly and monthly reports reviewed and format locked.
Step 4

Tune + document

Alert thresholds tuned with real data — the session-balance flag point, what counts as a red flag — and the full audit routine documented as an SOP so the process is transparent and repeatable.

Gate: audit SOP delivered; thresholds signed off.
